Geophysical Projects

  • Concepción del Oro, Zacatecas: Conducted geophysical surveys using Induced Polarization (IP) methods to analyze subsurface properties and identify potential mineral deposits for mining operations.
  • El Rosario, Querétaro: Participated in an archaeological research internship with UNAM, employing magnetometry, seismic refraction, Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT), gravimetry, and H/V methods to study subsurface structures.
  • Querétaro, Querétaro: Executed geophysical investigations for archaeological purposes in a residential area, applying magnetometry, seismic refraction, and ERT to identify buried structures.
  • Mexico City, República de Perú Street: Conducted Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) surveys as part of archaeological research focusing on Mexica culture, identifying subsurface artifacts and features.
  • Metztitlán, Hidalgo: Performed Vertical Electrical Sounding (VES) to evaluate structural conditions and determine the depth of an anodic bed for engineering applications.
  • Ecatepec, State of Mexico: Applied VES techniques to perform structural analyses and estimate the depth of anodic beds, contributing to construction planning and geotechnical studies.
  • Zinapécuaro, Michoacán: Conducted combined VES and Audio-Magnetotelluric (AMT) surveys to assess groundwater potential and evaluate the feasibility of drilling a new aquifer well.
